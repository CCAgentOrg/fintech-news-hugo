# Weekly Digital Governance Newsletter — Digital India Archiver

**Week of March 3-8, 2026**

---

## 1. Weekly Overview

This week's digital governance landscape in India sees significant movement on multiple fronts: UIDAI continues expanding Aadhaar's reach with new offline verification capabilities while facing ongoing privacy scrutiny; India's fintech ecosystem prepares for major IPO activity with PhonePe targeting a $9-10.5 billion valuation; the government amends IT Rules to address deepfakes; and the Union Budget 2026-27 introduces tax incentives for data center investments. The intersection of digital identity, financial inclusion, and AI regulation continues to shape policy discourse.

---

## 2. Aadhaar/UIDAI Updates

### Offline Aadhaar Verification Expands

UIDAI has strengthened Aadhaar's offline verification capabilities:
- **Offline eKYC XML**: Aadhaar holders can now generate XML files for offline verification without biometrics, allowing businesses to verify identity without real-time connectivity
- **m-Aadhaar Integration**: The mobile app now supports offline QR code generation for verification purposes
- **Paperless eKYC**: API-based verification continues to expand across banking, telecom, and government services

### Aadhaar Update Process 2026

UIDAI has announced that the free online Aadhaar update facility has been extended until **June 14, 2026**. Users can update their address and upload supporting documents without fees through the myAadhaar portal.([source](https://aadharcarddownloads.org/blog/aadhaar-card-update-and-correction-form/))

## 3. Digital Public Infrastructure News

### PhonePe IPO Preparation

Walmart-backed PhonePe is finalizing plans for its much-anticipated IPO:
- **Valuation Target**: $9-10.5 billion
- **Expected Timing**: April 2026, subject to market conditions
- **Key Stakeholders**: Walmart trimming ~12% stake; Tiger Global and Microsoft looking to exit
- **Capital Raising**: Aiming for approximately $1.05 billion

This would be one of the largest Indian fintech IPOs, reflecting confidence in the UPI-based digital payments ecosystem.([source](https://www.moneycontrol.com/news/business/personal-finance/rbi-proposes-new-rules-to-protect-customers-from-digital-banking-fraud-from-july-1-13853276.html))([source](https://www.reuters.com/business/retail-consumer/walmart-backed-phonepe-targets-up-105-billion-valuation-india-ipo-sources-say-2026-03-04/))

### Apple Pay India Entry

Apple is actively exploring bringing Apple Pay to the Indian market:
- Engaging with NPCI for UPI integration
- Discussing compliance with RBI's digital payment regulations
- Potential timeline for launch under discussion

This follows similar moves by other global players like Revolut, which recently secured its India payments license.([source](https://www.finextra.com/newsarticle/47374/apple-looks-to-bring-apple-pay-to-india))

### AI in Healthcare Governance

Health Secretary Punya Salila Srivastava highlighted AI's potential to reduce the burden on India's healthcare workforce, emphasizing that digital health infrastructure must prioritize patient safety and data protection. This aligns with broader DPI (Digital Public Infrastructure) expansion in the health sector.([source](https://www.etvbharat.com/en/bharat/ai-has-potential-to-reduce-the-burden-on-the-healthcare-workforce-health-secretary-enn26021605250))

## 4. Data Protection & Privacy Developments

### IT Rules Amended for Deepfake Regulation

India has amended the IT Rules 2021 to bring deepfakes under a formal regulatory framework:
- **Mandatory labeling** of synthetically generated content
- **Traceability requirements** for AI-generated audio-visual content
- **3-hour deadline** for social media platforms to remove reported deepfakes
- **Fines and penalties** for non-compliance, extending to platform liability

The amendments follow increasing concerns about AI-generated misinformation during electoral processes.([source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/10/india-orders-social-media-platforms-to-take-down-deepfakes-faster/))

## 5. Policy Announcements

### Budget 2026: Data Center Tax Incentives

The Union Budget 2026-27 has introduced significant provisions for digital infrastructure:
- **100% tax deduction** for data center investments for first 3 years
- **Reduced MAT** (Minimum Alternate Tax) for data center companies
- **Customs duty exemptions** on key equipment imports

This is expected to accelerate India's AI data center capacity, which TCS estimates needs to reach 10 gigawatts by 2030.([source](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/ip-law/india-budget-paved-way-for-tech-investment-with-tax-certainty))

### GCC Policy Boost

The government announced measures to strengthen India's position as a global hub for Global Capability Centers (GCCs):
- Simplified regulatory framework for setting up GCCs
- Data localization exemptions for GCCs with adequate security standards
- Enhanced GIFT City infrastructure for fintech and tech GCCs

India is already home to over 1,700 GCCs employing 1.66 million professionals.([source](https://law.asia/global-capability-centres-india/))

### AI Skilling Initiative

IT Minister Ashwini Vaishnaw announced a government program to upskill and reskill the IT workforce to meet AI-related challenges, acknowledging the transformative impact of AI on employment and the need for proactive adaptation.([source](https://www.thehindu.com/sci-tech/technology/government-to-upskill-reskill-it-workforce-amid-ai-challenges-ashwini-vaishnaw/article70650559.ece))
